Corporate training is broken. We're fixing it.

Despite billions invested annually, completion rates remain abysmal and employees don't retain or apply what they learn. The reason? Training hasn't evolved for the modern workforce.

Different learning styles, varied roles, and rapid skill obsolescence demand personalization at scale. One-size-fits-all simply doesn't work anymore. Yet most platforms are built for large enterprises with dedicated L&D teams and six-figure budgets — leaving everyone else behind.

Our Story

Why We Built Humaloom

We started Humaloom because we saw the same pattern everywhere: HR teams that cared deeply about their people but couldn't deliver the development those people deserved. Not because of a lack of effort or ambition — because of a lack of resources.

We've been in those rooms. We've heard the budget conversations. We've watched talented HR professionals try to patch together training from free YouTube videos and outdated binders because that's all they had. Meanwhile, larger companies were spending millions on personalized learning platforms their employees actually used and benefited from.

That gap didn't sit right with us. Every employee — whether they work at a 50-person retail chain or a Fortune 500 — deserves learning that actually fits them. So we built Humaloom to make that possible. AI-powered personalization using the content companies already have, at a price point that doesn't require a boardroom debate. Because great development shouldn't be a privilege reserved for those with the biggest budgets.

What Drives Us

Three principles that shape every decision we make.

Personalization

Every employee deserves learning that fits their needs, their role, and their goals. Cookie-cutter training is a thing of the past.

Accessibility

Enterprise-level L&D shouldn't require an enterprise budget. We're here to level the playing field for teams of every size.

Impact

Training should measurably improve business performance. If it's not moving the needle, it's not working. We obsess over outcomes.
